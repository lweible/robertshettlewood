Timberland

In Fall of 2021, I Interned as a brand manager at Timberland. My responsibility was to establish a connection between the company and its college-aged consumers and formulate a plan to push the brand’s values to the forefront of their identity. My team decided to focus on changing the perception that students at NYU had of their local Timberland flagship store. We decided this would be a good area to start building connections in after I conducted a qualtrics survey that captured data from nearly 1,200 NYU students. The survey found that 65% of respondents had never visited their local flagship location and 40% of those who had visited the store reported not purchasing any products. This information shaped our goal: to make the shopping experience fun, interactive and informative.

We strategized “A Night in the Woods”, an after-hours game night and private shopping experience with live music from DJs that attended NYU’s music business school. The games included environmental trivia with the opportunity to spin the prize wheel. Students could win discounts, Timberland apparel, and Timberland outdoor accessories.
